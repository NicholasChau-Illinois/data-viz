2008 Financial Crisis and Global GDP

GDP Over Time

This visualization shows the global trend of GDP over time. The general trend for GDP is that it keeps increasing making the Financial Crisis of 2008 a big outlier in an otherwise stable graph.

Decrease in GDP by Region (2008-2009)

This chart displays the decrease in GDP by region between 2008 and 2009. There are also more quantifiers to demonstrate how the financial crisis hit harder for certain types of demographics. For example, the biggest losses comes from those of high income where as the ones from low income were negligibly affected. This can also be seen in the next slide if you select a country that is classified as a third world country.

GDP over Time by Country

Select any country,region, or the world to see its GDP trend. The 2008 financial crisis may have affected those of high income disproportionately, but those of poorer countries still face many economic turmoils.
